How the computer limit works

Each licence covers a set number of computers. The first time the application runs on a machine it claims one place, and after that it checks nothing — the confirmation is stored locally and verified without the internet, so the application keeps working offline and keeps working if our servers do not.

Changing computers is expected. Replacing a laptop does not cost you a place permanently. Release the old machine from within the application, or, if you no longer have it, release it from the new one — you will be shown the list and asked which to free. A computer we have not heard from in six months releases its place on its own.

What is sent. Claiming a place sends a one-way fingerprint of the computer, a name you can recognise it by, and the date. Not the computer's identity, not your address book, not anything you ship. The privacy policy spells this out.

What is not included

Postage. Easy-Post Desktop does not sell postage and takes no cut of it. Labels are bought through your own EasyPost account at whatever rates that account is entitled to, and EasyPost bills you directly. You will need an EasyPost account before the application is of any use.

Carrier accounts. Any carrier accounts, negotiated rates or contracts remain between you, EasyPost and the carrier.
