Privacy policy

Last updated 21 July 2026

This policy explains what Easy-Post Desktop stores, what it transmits and to whom. The data controller is Spencer Fields, a sole trader established in London, United Kingdom, contactable at AppleApps@spencerfields.com.

The short version

Shipping data stays on your own computer. The application contains no analytics, no telemetry, no advertising identifiers and no crash reporting. Nothing is transmitted to the Supplier by the application at any time — not on launch, not on purchase of a label, not ever.

What the application stores on your computer

  • Your EasyPost API keys, held in the operating system credential store — Windows Credential Manager or the macOS Keychain — and never written to a plain file.
  • A local database containing the address book, shipment and tracking history, batch records, claims, pickups and the tariff-code cache. This lives in the standard per-user application data directory on your machine.
  • Local preferences, such as the chosen language and label size, and the licence key if one has been activated.

None of this is synchronised, backed up or uploaded anywhere by the application. Removing the application and its data directory removes it all.

What the application sends, and where

  • EasyPost. Address, parcel, customs, tracking, insurance, pickup and claim data is sent to EasyPost's API in order to perform the action requested, using your own EasyPost account. EasyPost is an independent data controller for that data, governed by its own privacy policy.
  • The U.S. International Trade Commission. When the tariff-code lookup is used, the search term alone is sent to the Commission's public database. No shipment or personal data accompanies it.
  • Carrier label images. Purchased label files are fetched from the address EasyPost provides so they can be displayed and saved.
  • Cloudflare, only if you enable real-time tracking. The optional webhook feature is off by default. Turning it on starts a local receiver and a Cloudflare tunnel so that EasyPost can deliver tracking events to your machine. It can be turned off again at any time in Settings.

Buying a licence

Purchases are handled by Paddle.com Market Ltd, our authorised reseller and Merchant of Record. Payment details are collected and processed entirely by Paddle. The Supplier never sees, receives or stores card numbers or bank details.

Paddle passes the Supplier the purchaser's email address and the order reference, which are used to generate and send the licence key. Paddle's handling of personal data is governed by its own privacy notice.

The licence key email is delivered through Resend, an email delivery provider, which processes the recipient address for that purpose alone.

Purchaser email addresses and order references are retained for as long as necessary to support the licence and to meet accounting obligations. They are never sold, rented or used for marketing.

This website

These pages are static. No cookies are set, no analytics or tracking scripts run, and no visitor accounts exist. Web fonts are loaded from Google Fonts, which receives the requesting IP address as an ordinary consequence of serving the font files. Standard server access logs are kept by the hosting provider for security and diagnostic purposes.

Correspondence

Email or telephone contact for support or a refund is retained only for as long as needed to deal with the matter and to keep a record of it.

Your rights

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation and equivalent laws, you may request access to the personal data held about you, ask for it to be corrected or erased, object to its processing, or request a copy in portable form. Write to AppleApps@spencerfields.com and the request will be answered within one month.

Data held about a purchase is limited to the email address, order reference and licence key. Shipping data is not held by the Supplier at all, so a request concerning it should be directed to EasyPost.

A complaint may be raised with the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

Children

Easy-Post Desktop is a business tool and is not directed at children. Personal data is not knowingly collected from anyone under sixteen.

Changes
